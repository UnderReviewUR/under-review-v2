Hi — welcome to Under Review.

**What UR Take is actually doing**

Sportsbooks post odds. Those odds imply a win probability — the market's "price" on an outcome. UR Take compares that to the story the data tells: injuries, matchup, usage, pace, and how similar spots have played out before.

When our side of the story **lines up materially better** than what the odds imply, that's what we mean by an **edge**. You're not paying for a guaranteed winner; you're paying for analysis that aims to find spots where the **price is wrong**, not where the game is easy to predict.

**Quick example**

Say the prop market prices Shai Gilgeous-Alexander's points line like he's a ~28 PPG scorer tonight, but the matchup, role, and recent shot volume point toward **31+**. If we flag SGA over 30.5 as the play, we're not saying "lock" — we're saying the **line understates** how likely the over is relative to a fair price. That's the edge: your probability vs the market's implied probability.

## Email 2 — Day 2

**Subject line:** Why we don't hit 100% (and why that's OK)

**Preview text (optional):** One loss doesn't erase math — here's why.

---

Hi — quick reality check that every sharp bettor lives with.

**Variance**

Even when you have an edge, **short-term results bounce around**. You can lose a coin flip that's weighted 55/45 in your favor — and you will, often. Sports outcomes are messy: shooting luck, whistles, one bad quarter. That noise is called **variance**. It doesn't mean the process was wrong; it means one game isn't the whole story.

**Long-run frequency**

What matters over time is whether **your wins at a given price** match or beat what the odds implied. Think of it as **long-run frequency**: over dozens of similar bets, do you come out ahead of break-even? That's how +EV shows up — not in every ticket, but **across the set**.

**Example**

You take SGA over 30.5 because the market is sleeping on his usage in a fast-paced game. He could finish with 29 on a quiet foul-trouble night and the bet loses. That's **one outcome**. The question for edge isn't that night alone — it's whether that **type** of bet wins often enough at that price to profit over many tries.

Hi — you're past the basics. Here's how to read UR Take like a sharp.

**Implied probability — in plain English**

Every price implies odds of winning. Roughly: favorites cost more because the market thinks they happen more often; dogs pay more because they're expected to lose more often. UR Take spells out where **our view of the win chance** diverges from **what the posted line implies**. That's the heart of the card.

**What "finding an edge" feels like in the app**

You should see **why** the number is off — not just a pick. Example: SGA over 30.5 — play because the market is pricing him closer to a 28 PPG night when injury reports, pace, and shot profile say **31+ is more realistic**. You're looking for that gap: **your story vs the market's implied story**.
